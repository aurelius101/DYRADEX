001
Cheepit

It uses its rock-hard head to crush nuts and seeds.

Insatiably curious, Cheepit will investigate anything that catches its fancy,
often destroying it in the process.

Its head is heavier than its body, so when it gets too excited, it’s prone to tipping over.

002
Raptanin

These Pokemon have a rebellious streak and will challenge their own trainer to a battle if riled up.

In the wild, flock hierarchy is determined by whichever individual has the most impressive crest.

003
Kotylodon

It uses its bright warning plumage in threat display. It frequently picks fights.

It is fiercely territorial and will rip up plants and trees that it perceives to be invading its habitat.
Seeds that fall from its feathers are harder than any known stone.

004
Inkle

These Pokémon used to be often seen floating in water in large groups. Large group sightings of these Pokémon may often signify an incoming storm.

Inkle are social and bond very quickly with others.

005
Jellink

These Pokemon are seen much more solitary than Inkle and separate from their groups.

They walk on their powerful tentacles to seem bigger and more intimidating.

006
Medumbra

Medumbra are known as aggressive, volatile Pokemon, with their aggression deemed territorial. They are solitary, much like Jellinks, and pack a powerful punch.

These Pokemon can "float" for small periods of time.

007
Mongrowl

It disperses heat through its large ears.
The intensity and heat of the flames indicate Mongrowl’s emotions.

Its flames never goes out, just like its loyalty.

008
Scorchound

As Scorchound matures, it displays erratic and aggressive behaviour. Its own tail begins vaguely resembling a serpent as it gains its own separate conscience.

Scorchound will often seek solitude, afraid of accidentally harming its Trainer.

009
Howlfire

Despite the intense and dangerous appearance of its flames, Howlfire has it under control.

The more close it is with its Trainer, the hotter its flames burn.
